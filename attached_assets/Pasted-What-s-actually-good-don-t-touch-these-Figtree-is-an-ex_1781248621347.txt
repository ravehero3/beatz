Figtree is an excellent modern font choice — tight tracking on headings feels intentional
The glassmorphic navbar (backdrop-filter: blur(12px)) is a solid start
Pill buttons and consistent 16px card radius are cohesive
The procedurally-generated waveform per beat is genuinely clever
Three audio player variants (Classic/Minimal/Deck) shows real product thinking
What's missing or dated — here's the honest breakdown
1. Glassmorphism stops at the navbar
You have glass in one place. In 2026 it's a systemic effect — modals, cards, the audio player, sidebars, purchase drawers should all be frosted glass panels (backdrop-filter: blur(20px), rgba(255,255,255,0.7)). Right now the rest of the app is flat white with border lines, which feels like 2020.

2. The bottom player is a missed opportunity
A full-width deep black bar at the bottom is the old SoundCloud / Spotify approach. The trend now (Apple Music, Arc browser, Notion AI) is a floating glass pill that hovers above the content — border-radius: 20px, backdrop-filter: blur(24px), soft ambient glow from the track's cover art color underneath it. Yours is a solid black rectangle — huge visual hierarchy clash on a white-bg app.

3. Flat white background with zero atmosphere
Every major 2026 white-bg product (Linear, Vercel, Notion) has a subtle background atmosphere: a very soft radial gradient in a corner, an almost-invisible noise texture, or a 4–6% saturation color tint. Yours is #FFFFFF blank — clinically flat. Even a 2% warm tint changes everything.

4. No color in shadows — all shadows are grey
This is one of the specific "Google effect" things you mentioned. Google Material 3 and modern Apple UI use colored shadows — a card's shadow is tinted to the card's content color, usually at 15–20% opacity. For BeatCards with cover art, this would mean the shadow picks up a hint of the artwork color. Currently all shadows are rgba(0,0,0,0.08) — flat, lifeless, 2018.

5. BeatCard is functional but has no soul
Current: flat card, flat image, metadata text, basic hover lift. Missing:

Image bloom — a very subtle blurred copy of the cover art behind the card that bleeds into the background (like Spotify's now-playing screen)
Glass metadata strip — the BPM/Key/Genre tags could be a frosted pill overlay on the image
Hover state depth — should feel like the card lifts into the screen, not just translate-Y
6. No page transitions or entrance animations
Content just... appears. In 2026, beats grid loading should stagger-fade in, cards should spring in sequentially, filters should animate. No skeleton loaders either — just blank space then content.

7. Typography has one gear
Headings are bold and tight-tracked (good), but every page uses the same formula. Missing:

A display size for the hero (should be enormous, like 80–100px on desktop)
Gradient text on 1–2 key headlines (very subtle — just a 20° linear gradient from #0A0A0A to #444)
Numeric counters that animate on scroll for stats like plays / artists / beats
8. Primary CTA buttons have no energy
The pill buttons are clean but lifeless. Missing:

A subtle inner highlight (a 1px white line at the top edge of dark buttons creates instant depth)
Shimmer on hover — a light sweep across the button surface
The buy/license buttons on BeatCards should have a gradient fill, not flat black
9. Radix modals and dialogs are plain white boxes
The purchase flow, license picker — these open in basic white modals. These should be the most glassmorphic surfaces in the app since they float above content. Heavy blur, slight tint, soft shadow ring.

10. Empty states are probably just text
When there are no featured beats or search returns nothing — most apps just render empty space or plain "no results" text. In 2026 this should be a thoughtful illustrated/animated empty state.
